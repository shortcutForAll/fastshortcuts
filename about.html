About FastShortcuts

We're developers who got tired of googling "how to refactor in IntelliJ" for the 47th time. So we built this.

The Origin Story

FastShortcuts started from pure frustration. You know that moment when you're deep in flow state, fingers flying across the keyboard, and then... you have to grab your mouse to click through three menus just to rename a variable?

Yeah, we got tired of that too.

As developers, we knew keyboard shortcuts existed for almost everything. The problem? They were scattered across outdated documentation, buried in 200-page PDFs, or locked behind video tutorials where you had to scrub through 10 minutes to find one shortcut.

So in late 2024, we decided to fix this. We started compiling shortcuts for our own use - VS Code, IntelliJ, Windows, Mac - tools we used every day. Then we thought: why not share this with everyone?

FastShortcuts was born from that simple idea: one centralized, searchable database of keyboard shortcuts for developers. No ads, no paywalls, no BS. Just shortcuts.

Why Keyboard Shortcuts Matter

Here's the thing about shortcuts: they're not just about speed (though that's a nice bonus). They're about maintaining focus.

Studies show it takes 23 minutes to fully recover from an interruption. Every time you stop to google a shortcut, you're not just losing those 30 seconds - you're potentially losing 20+ minutes of deep work.

Keyboard shortcuts let you stay in the zone. Your hands never leave the keyboard. Your eyes stay on the code. Your brain stays in flow state. That's when the magic happens.
